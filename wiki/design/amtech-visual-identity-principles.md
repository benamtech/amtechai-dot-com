# AMTECH Visual Identity Principles

## Brand premise

AMTECH teaches ambitious small and medium business operators how to install AI into the practical work of the business.

## Visual premise

A 1970s industrial field manual crossed with a modern AI operating system.

## Emotional premise

Calm authority. No hype. No softness. The future is here; serious operators learn the system.

## Content premise

Every article is a specific business procedure, not a thought-leadership essay.

## Principles

### 1. The grid is the machine

Use strong desktop grids, hard alignments, and explicit section breaks. Do not center everything. Let the structure communicate competence.

### 2. The red period is the signal

AMTECH red `#E11D2A` is the system signal. Use it for punctuation, labels, active states, proof accents, CTA blocks, and critical emphasis. Avoid orange and miscellaneous accents unless a semantic exception is documented.

### 3. Typography carries authority

Use large compressed display headlines for doctrine and concise body copy for instruction. Mono labels orient the reader like machine tags or document metadata.

### 4. Symbols explain action

Symbols are operating marks, not decorative icons. Each pictogram must clarify a task, object, role, or system state.

### 5. Every page is a field document

A page should feel like a useful artifact: manual, operating bulletin, application notice, briefing, system diagram, or procedure sheet.

### 6. Education pages are operating procedures

An education page must tell the operator what changes in the business this week. It should include inputs, steps, review points, risks, outputs, and next action.

### 7. CTAs are applications, briefings, or assignments

Avoid generic CTA language. Use precise action frames: apply for access, book an operator briefing, run the checklist, review the system, start the procedure.

### 8. No generic AI imagery

Do not use abstract stock technology imagery as proof. Prefer dashboards, transcripts, workflow strips, operating diagrams, checklists, symbols, and system outputs.

### 9. Specificity beats inspiration

The more specific the industry, task, role, input, output, and risk, the more authoritative AMTECH becomes.

### 10. The operator is the hero; AI is the instrument

AMTECH should never imply business owners are being replaced. AI is the instrument that performs repetitive work under operator direction.

## Palette

- Black: `#0a0a0a`
- White: `#ffffff`
- AMTECH red: `#E11D2A`
- Utility gray: `#6b6b6b` / `#6b7280`
- Field gray: `#f4f4f4` / `#f5f5f5`
- Success green: reserved for completed machine status only

## Type hierarchy

- Manifesto: huge display type, negative tracking, short declarative copy.
- Procedure: readable body copy with tight line length.
- Machine label: mono uppercase, small, high tracking.
- Evidence: oversized number or status mark.
- Caption: small gray explanatory text.

## Layout rules

- Use a 12-column desktop grid where possible.
- Use narrow body columns for teaching.
- Use wide diagram panels for workflows.
- Use thick editorial section breaks when the mode changes.
- Use index-like cards for hubs, article clusters, and related procedures.
