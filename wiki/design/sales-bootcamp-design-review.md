# Sales Bootcamp Design Review

## Role in the system

`/sales-bootcamp` is the strongest current reference for high-conviction educational conversion. It should become the canonical rhythm for future AMTECH education pages.

## Why it works

- The hero makes a sharp promise and immediately names the system.
- The dark problem section creates contrast and explains why old behavior fails.
- The offer section uses a hard editorial rule, AMTECH mark, red label, and checklist structure.
- The daily rhythm section turns education into repeated behavior.
- The curriculum makes the sequence tangible.
- The outcome quote provides a doctrine/proof panel.
- The fit / not-fit section qualifies the operator.
- The scarlet CTA resolves the page into a direct next action.

## Reusable sequence

1. Hero promise
2. Problem diagnosis
3. System explanation
4. Daily or weekly operating rhythm
5. Curriculum / procedure / sequence
6. Proof or outcome panel
7. For / not-for qualification
8. CTA

## Adaptation for education-first articles

Future article pages should use this rhythm instead of generic blog layouts:

1. What problem exists?
2. Why the old workflow fails?
3. What AI workflow replaces it?
4. What does the operator do?
5. What does the machine do?
6. What outcome should happen?
7. What should the operator implement next?
