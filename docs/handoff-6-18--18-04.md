### Claim Verification Flow

Acceptance criteria implemented:

- First form block collects name, AI Employee name, phone, timezone, and owner/operator checkbox.
- A separate verification block appears immediately after the first block.
- The verification code is entered and verified in that block before the business questions.
- The business info block comes after phone verification.
- The final `Claim it` block contains only consent and the final claim button.
- The final button starts claim/provisioning; it does not ask for or verify a code.

Flow:

1. `/claim/send-code` sends Twilio Verify OTP.
2. `/claim/verify-code` checks OTP and returns a short-lived signed `claim_token`.
3. `/claim/verify-and-claim` accepts `claim_token`, consent, and answers, then persists the claim and triggers provisioning.
4. SMS-first links already arrive with a signed token, so they skip code verification and still require the form + consent + final claim.

### Twilio

- Twilio Verify proves phone ownership for web claims.
- Twilio Messaging sends inbound SMS webhooks to `/sms-entry`.
- `/sms-entry` must validate `X-Twilio-Signature` using `TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN` and exact `TWILIO_SMS_WEBHOOK_URL`.
- SMS-first claim links are HMAC-signed with `CLAIM_LINK_SECRET`.
- Per-client Twilio numbers are claimed by `claim_number.py` from a managed pool.
- `RESERVED_NUMBERS` or `state/number-pool.json.reserved` prevents the onboarding number from being recycled into the per-client pool.

### Supabase

- Browser uses public `VITE_SUPABASE_URL` and `VITE_SUPABASE_ANON_KEY`.
- Netlify server functions use `SUPABASE_URL` and `SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY`.
- Active public conversion tables:

- `ai_employee_inbound_tokens` makes SMS/web claim tokens single-use where Supabase service-role env is configured.
